Major Cities

Mogadishu

The capital and largest city of Somalia, known for its beautiful beaches and historic architecture.

Hargeisa

Capital of Somaliland with vibrant markets and cool climate due to its high elevation.

Kismayo

Port city in southern Somalia known for its banana exports and beautiful coastline.

Rich Somali Culture

Poetry and Storytelling

Somalia is often referred to as a "nation of poets" with a rich oral tradition dating back centuries.

Clan System

The Somali society is organized around a complex clan system that plays a significant role in culture and politics.

Traditional Attire

Somali women often wear dirac (a long, light dress) while men wear macawis (a sarong-like garment).

Delicious Somali Cuisine

Staple Foods

Canjeero (similar to injera), rice, pasta, and meat (especially goat, lamb and camel).

Sambusa

Somali version of samosa, stuffed with meat or vegetables and spiced with cumin and coriander.

Camel Milk

A Somali staple, often consumed fresh or used to make sweets like halwa.

Sports in Somalia

Football (Soccer)

The most popular sport in Somalia, with the national team known as the Ocean Stars.

Basketball

Growing in popularity, especially among the youth in urban areas.

Traditional Sports

Shax (a traditional board game) and camel/horse racing are also enjoyed.

Fun Facts About Somalia

Longest Coastline

Somalia has Africa's longest coastline (3,025 km) along the Indian Ocean.

Ancient History

The Land of Punt mentioned by ancient Egyptians was likely in Somalia.

Camels

Somalia has more camels than any other country in the world.

Language

Somali is the official language, written in the Latin alphabet since 1972.
